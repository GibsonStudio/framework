Introduction

Mans early attempts at powered flight were thwarted by the lack of a suitable engine to provide the necessary power.

The Steam Engine
Steam Engines are very inefficient.

The steam engine, which was invented in the early 1700 was heavy and inefficient. Because combustion took place outside of the engine it was termed an ‘external combustion engine’. Over 90% of the heat energy produced in the steam engine was wasted.

The Internal Combustion Engine

In the late 19th century Dr Nicolaus August Otto succeeded in producing a working engine based on the internal combustion principle. The engine operates by inducing a mixture of air and fuel into a cylinder, through an inlet valve, which is automatically opened and closed at the appropriate times.

  • Induction
  • Compression
  • Ignition
  • Exhaust

The mixture is then compressed by a piston.

The mixture is ignited by an electrical spark.

The rapid rise in temperature causes the gas pressure in the cylinder to rise forcing the piston down the cylinder.

Linear movement of the piston is converted into rotary motion by a connecting rod and crankshaft.

Categories of Internal Combustion Engine

Internal Combustion Engines have developed into three main categories:

  • Compression ignition or diesel engines.
  • Wankel rotary engines.
  • Two and four stroke spark ignition engines.
